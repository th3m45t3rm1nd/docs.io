**Status:** Accepted  
**Decides:** Geographic scope of launch, and how the schema handles future expansion

## Context

The platform plans to launch in Delhi NCR and later expand to other Indian cities (Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, etc.).

Two schema approaches were considered:

1. **Launch without city awareness** — no `city_id` on products or services. Add it later when needed.
    
2. **Build city-awareness into the schema now** — add `city_id` to key tables from day one, even though only one city is active at launch.
    

---

## Decision

**Launch in Delhi NCR only, but build city-awareness into the schema from day one.**

The `cities` table is created at launch. All products, supplier listings, and service listings carry a `city_id`. At launch, the only value is `delhi-ncr`. When Mumbai launches, one row is inserted into `cities` and suppliers onboard for that city.

---

## Reasoning

Adding `city_id` to tables after launch — when there are thousands of product listings, orders, and supplier accounts — is a painful migration. Column additions require backfills, backfills require downtime windows or careful online migration scripts, and there is always a risk of incorrect data if the backfill logic is wrong.

Adding the column at launch costs nothing. It is always `delhi-ncr` for the first year and is ignored by all queries until a second city is ready.

The `city_id` column also enables city-specific features immediately: trending products per city, city-specific promotional banners, supplier coverage maps.

## Delhi NCR specific: the three-state problem

"Delhi" for users means a region that spans three Indian states:

|Area|State|State code|
|---|---|---|
|Delhi|Delhi|07|
|Noida, Greater Noida, Ghaziabad|Uttar Pradesh|09|
|Gurgaon, Faridabad|Haryana|06|

This matters because:

**GST:** Moving goods across state lines (Delhi → Noida is Delhi → UP) triggers IGST instead of CGST + SGST. The `pincodes` reference table stores `state_code` for every pincode. The order service compares origin warehouse `state_code` against destination pincode `state_code` to determine which GST type applies.

**E-way bills:** Required for interstate goods movement above ₹50,000 in value. A delivery from a Delhi supplier to a Noida construction site for ₹60,000 of cement requires an e-way bill generated before the truck moves. The order service handles this automatically. See the e-way bill runbook for manual intervention procedures.

**Service provider licensing:** A contractor or plumber licensed in Delhi may have restrictions working in Haryana or UP. This is currently handled by requiring suppliers and service providers to declare their operating pincodes explicitly (the `service_areas` table). It is not enforced legally by the platform — that is the provider's responsibility.

## Pincode reference table

The `pincodes` table is seeded from India Post data and is the canonical source for state, district, and city mapping. User-entered addresses must be validated against this table. Free-text state entry is not permitted — the state is always derived from the pincode.

Delhi NCR pincodes at launch:

- Delhi: 110001–110096
- Noida / Greater Noida: 201301–201310
- Gurgaon: 122001–122018
- Faridabad: 121001–121010
- Ghaziabad: 201001–201015

(Full list in `src/db/seeds/pincodes.sql`)

## GRAP / seasonal restrictions

Delhi NCR is subject to Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP) restrictions during winter months (typically Oct–Feb) under orders from the Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM).

Under GRAP Stage III and IV:

- Construction activity may be banned or restricted
- Commercial vehicles may face entry restrictions into Delhi
- Certain building materials (fly ash bricks, stone crushers) face movement restrictions

The platform does not automatically enforce GRAP restrictions (they change frequently and vary by product type). Operations must manually update supplier availability and communicate delays to customers during active GRAP restrictions.

A GRAP status flag on the `cities` table can be used to display a banner to users when restrictions are active.

## Consequences

- `city_id` is a non-nullable foreign key on `products`, `supplier_products`, `service_listings`, and `warehouses` from day one
- All product queries must include `WHERE city_id = $city` — this is enforced by a shared query helper, not left to individual developers
- The pincodes seed file must be kept up to date as India Post changes pincode assignments (rare but happens)
- E-way bill generation adds latency to order confirmation for high-value interstate orders — handled asynchronously in the `order-confirm` worker, not in the checkout API
